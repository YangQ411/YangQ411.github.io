**Abstract:** Large Language Models (LLMs) have achieved strong performance across a wide range of natural language processing tasks, but their large parameter sizes make full fine-tuning computationally expensive. Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT), particularly Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA), has emerged as an effective approach for adapting LLMs to specific tasks. However, a fundamental question remains unresolved: where should LoRA be applied within model architectures to achieve the best performance–efficiency trade-off? This thesis presents a systematic study of LoRA placement strategies across five representative LLM configurations: Llama3.2-1B, Llama3.2-3B, Gemma3-1B, Qwen3-0.6B, and Falcon-1B. A comprehensive set of placement configurations is evaluated on multiple-choice question answering and instruction-following tasks under a unified experimental framework. The results show that adapting core computational modules, particularly attention, consistently yields the best efficiency–performance trade-off, whereas broader adaptation, such as full-layer LoRA, can degrade model effectiveness. Attention-only placement emerges as the most robust strategy across models, achieving strong accuracy gains with minimal parameter overhead. Beyond empirical evaluation, this thesis analyzes LoRA behavior from both weight-space and representation-space perspectives. Effective LoRA adaptation operates through structured and localized updates that induce moderate, well-propagated representation changes. In contrast, ineffective strategies either produce excessive but unstructured perturbations, as in embedding adaptation, or insufficient internal modification, as in output projection adaptation. These findings indicate that LoRA effectiveness is governed not only by the magnitude of parameter updates, but also by their structural alignment with the model’s computational pathways. Overall, this study provides practical guidance for LoRA placement and a principled understanding of parameter-efficient adaptation in large language models.
