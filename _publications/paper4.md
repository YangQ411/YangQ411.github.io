**Abstract:** Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) has become a widely used framework for aligning language models with human preferences, but its training dynamics can be sensitive to the choice of the inverse-temperature parameter and the resulting preference margin scale. This work proposes Learning-Signal-Controlled Direct Preference Optimization (LSC-DPO), a dynamic alignment method that controls the effective learning signal during preference optimization. By regulating the scaled preference margin through a feedback-based update rule, LSC-DPO aims to improve training stability while preserving the core DPO objective. The method is analyzed from the perspective of learning-signal behavior and optimization stability, and is evaluated on preference-alignment benchmarks to study its effects on alignment quality and robustness.
