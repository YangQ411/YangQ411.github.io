I am a Master's student in Software Engineering Systems at Northeastern University, working toward research in artificial intelligence and large language models. I am interested in understanding how intelligent systems can be made more capable, efficient, and reliable, especially when they are used for reasoning-intensive tasks. Through my recent projects, I have been exploring large language models from both methodological and application-oriented perspectives, including efficient model adaptation, reinforcement learning, and AI-assisted scientific discovery. My long-term goal is to develop AI systems that are not only strong in performance, but also better understood, more controllable, and more useful for complex reasoning and scientific discovery.

Research Interests
======

Large Language Models
------

My research interests focus on the development, understanding, and optimization of large language models (LLMs), combining both theoretical insights and practical systems work. Specifically, I am interested in:

- **Efficient Large Language Models:** Developing post-training and optimization methods to improve the performance, efficiency, and usability of large language models.

- **Reinforcement Learning:** Studying reinforcement learning algorithms, including RLHF and related methods, from their mathematical structure to practical applications, with the goal of improving model alignment, instruction following, and general capabilities.

- **LLM Reasoning:** Investigating the reasoning ability of large language models, with the goal of developing principled mathematical criteria for reasoning processes. My current focus is on using RL-based methods to improve LLM reasoning performance.


AI for Science
------

- **AI for Scientific Discovery:** Applying language models, deep learning, and other AI methods to scientific problems, especially in mathematics and theoretical physics, with the goal of providing new tools, perspectives, and methods for scientific reasoning and discovery.
